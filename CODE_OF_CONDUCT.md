# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Hermit is intended to be a welcoming, high-signal project for builders, operators, researchers, and contributors with different backgrounds and experience levels.

We want this repository to be a place where people can:

- ask questions without embarrassment
- disagree on technical direction without personal attacks
- share ideas, prototypes, and critiques in good faith
- contribute docs, design, code, tests, and operational insight safely

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that help this community:

- being respectful, patient, and specific
- assuming good intent while still giving honest technical feedback
- focusing criticism on ideas, tradeoffs, and implementation details
- sharing context, evidence, and reproduction steps when reporting issues
- helping newcomers understand project direction and constraints

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, threats, intimidation, or discrimination
- sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention
- personal attacks, insults, or deliberately demeaning comments
- publishing private information without permission
- trolling, bad-faith disruption, or sustained hostility in issues, pull requests, or discussions
- deliberately misrepresenting security issues, benchmarks, or project status

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within the repository and in project-linked spaces such as issues, pull requests, discussions, and other official community channels.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, discussions, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers are expected to enforce these standards fairly, document important moderation decisions when appropriate, and prioritize the safety of contributors and users.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

- for general conduct concerns, contact the maintainer through GitHub
- for security-sensitive concerns, follow [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md) instead of opening a public issue

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate in response to unacceptable behavior, including warnings, comment removal, temporary restrictions, or permanent bans from project spaces.
